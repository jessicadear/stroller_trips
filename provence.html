    Aigues Mortes

    is a striking, walled Medieval town sitting on the flat marshes of the Petite Camargue, and is considered the purest example of 13th-century military architecture. The town of neatly rectilinear streets is surrounded by a crenelated wall with 5 towers and 10 fortified portes. Come and discover the origin of the pink water..

    See to believe

    Lavender

    is definitely not only in breathtaking patings. Here in Provence, you can walk around fields, museums, and even take home a piece of this purple paradise.

    See, smell, wear lavender

    Château d'If

    is a fortress (later a prison) located on the island of If, the smallest island in the Frioul archipelago situated in the Mediterranean Sea about a mile offshore in the Bay of Marseille in southeastern France. It is famous for being one of the settings of Alexandre Dumas' adventure novel The Count of Monte Cristo.

    Must see!

    Truffles

    are also called "the diamond of the kitchen" and are frequently seen in international haute cuisine. Truffles are generally served raw, and shaved over steaming buttered pasta, salads, or fried eggs. White or black paper-thin truffle slices may be inserted into meats, under the skins of roasted fowl, in foie gras preparations, in pâtés, or in stuffings. Some speciality cheeses contain truffles, as well as truffle vodka and truffle oil.

    Want a piece?

    Roussillon

    is situated in the heart of the biggest ochre deposit in Europe, Roussillon blazons forth its difference through an astonishing palette of flaming colours. As you wander its narrow streets and stairways and gaze upon its facades, which are often quite plain and naturally beautiful, you will be admiring the result of age-old know-how.

    Live the colors

    Fontaine-de-Vaucluse

    is at the base of high, rocky cliffs, a deep pool of seemingly still water is actually a full-fledged river gushing up out of the depths. A few meters from this pool, the white water rapids crash down over black rocks, giving away the truth of the "still waters" of the source of the Sorgue river. Below the rapids, the river settles down to a wide expanse in front of dams and waterwheels, passing under the bridge at the center of the village, and then flowing on downstream as a lovely river.

    Splash away

    Tarascon

    is a medieval town that features 15th-century Roi Rene's chateau, the 12th-14th-century Saint Martha collegiate church and the 15th-century Saint Jacques church with its lovely Cordeliers cloister.

    Long live the king

    Carpentras

    is famous for the black truffle markets held from winter to early spring. Carpentras is also well known for various local delicacies, including the delicious Carpentras Candy hand made in the local shops.
